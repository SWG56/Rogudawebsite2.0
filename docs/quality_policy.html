1. Quality Policy Statement

Roguda Fashion & Art Design School is committed to providing world-class fashion education that meets and exceeds the standards set by the Quality Council for Trades and Occupations (QCTO) and the South African Qualifications Authority (SAQA).

Our Commitment: We ensure that every student receives quality education through rigorous assessment, qualified instructors, industry-relevant curriculum, and continuous improvement.

This Quality Policy applies to all programmes, courses, assessments, and student services offered by Roguda Fashion & Art Design School.

2. QCTO Accreditation & Compliance

2.1 Accreditation Status

Roguda Fashion & Art Design School is accredited by the Quality Council for Trades and Occupations (QCTO) to offer the following qualifications:

  • Fashion Design Qualification - NQF Level 5 (QCTO-FD-001)
  • Pattern Making & Grading - NQF Level 4 (QCTO-PM-002)
  • Fashion Production Management - NQF Level 5 (QCTO-FPM-003)

3. Assessment & Grading Standards

3.1 Assessment Principles

All assessments at Roguda are designed to be:

  • Valid: Assessments measure what they are intended to measure
  • Reliable: Consistent results across different assessors and occasions
  • Fair: Equitable treatment for all students regardless of background
  • Flexible: Accommodate diverse learning styles and needs
  • Authentic: Reflect real-world industry requirements

3.2 Grading Scale

Grade Percentage Description
Outstanding (A+) 90-100% Exceptional performance
Excellent (A) 80-89% Superior achievement
Good (B) 70-79% Above average
Satisfactory (C) 60-69% Competent performance
Pass (D) 50-59% Minimum competence
Fail (F) 0-49% Not yet competent

3.3 Assessment Methods

  • Formative Assessments: Ongoing feedback during learning process
  • Summative Assessments: End-of-module evaluations
  • Practical Assessments: Hands-on demonstration of skills
  • Portfolio Assessments: Collection of student work over time
  • Work-Integrated Learning: Industry placement evaluations

4. Educator Qualifications & Development

4.1 Minimum Requirements

All instructors must meet the following criteria:

  • Minimum NQF Level 6 qualification in relevant field
  • At least 3 years of industry experience
  • Registered with SAQA as an assessor
  • Commitment to ongoing professional development

4.2 Professional Development

Roguda invests in continuous educator development through:

  • Annual training workshops on new teaching methodologies
  • Industry immersion programmes
  • Peer review and collaborative teaching practices
  • Access to international fashion education conferences

5. Student Support Services

5.1 Academic Support

  • Tutorials: Additional support for struggling students
  • Mentorship: One-on-one guidance from industry professionals
  • Study Skills Workshops: Time management, research techniques
  • Peer Learning Groups: Collaborative study sessions

5.2 Career Services

  • Job placement assistance
  • Resume and portfolio development
  • Interview preparation
  • Graduate incubator programme
  • Industry networking events

5.3 Accessibility & Inclusion

We are committed to providing equal access to education for all students:

  • Reasonable accommodations for students with disabilities
  • Flexible learning options (online, hybrid, in-person)
  • Financial aid and bursary programmes
  • Multilingual support services

6. Facilities & Learning Resources

6.1 Infrastructure Standards

  • Design Studios: Minimum 5m¬≤ per student workspace
  • Sewing Labs: Industrial-grade machines (1:4 student ratio)
  • Pattern-Making Rooms: Digital plotting equipment
  • AI Fashion Studio: 3D design software and visualization tools
  • Library: 5,000+ fashion publications and digital resources

6.2 Health & Safety

All facilities comply with Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA):

  • Annual safety audits and equipment inspections
  • Fire safety systems and emergency protocols
  • First aid facilities and trained personnel
  • COVID-19 safety measures as per government regulations

7. Continuous Quality Improvement

7.1 Monitoring Mechanisms

  • Student Feedback: End-of-module evaluations
  • Industry Advisory Board: Quarterly curriculum review
  • Internal Audits: Annual quality assurance reviews
  • Graduate Outcome Tracking: Employment rates and career progression

7.2 Improvement Cycle

Roguda follows a Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) quality improvement model:

  1. Plan: Identify improvement areas through data analysis
  2. Do: Implement changes on a pilot basis
  3. Check: Evaluate effectiveness of changes
  4. Act: Standardize successful improvements

8. Academic Integrity & Ethics

8.1 Plagiarism Policy

Roguda has a zero-tolerance policy for academic dishonesty:

  • All work must be original and properly cited
  • Plagiarism detection software used for written assignments
  • First offense: Written warning and assignment resubmission
  • Repeat offense: Academic probation or expulsion

8.2 Intellectual Property

  • Students retain copyright to their design work
  • School may showcase student work for promotional purposes (with consent)
  • Industry collaborations governed by separate IP agreements

9. Complaints & Appeals Process

9.1 Complaint Submission

Students may lodge complaints regarding:

  • Assessment outcomes
  • Quality of instruction
  • Facilities and resources
  • Administrative services

9.2 Resolution Process

  1. Informal Resolution (5 days): Discuss with course coordinator
  2. Formal Complaint (10 days): Submit written complaint to Academic Dean
  3. Appeals Committee (15 days): Independent review by faculty panel
  4. External Review (30 days): QCTO mediation if unresolved
Confidentiality Guarantee: All complaints are handled with strict confidentiality. No student will face retaliation for lodging a legitimate complaint.

10. Certification & Quality Recognition

10.1 Graduation Requirements

To receive QCTO-accredited certification, students must:

  • Complete all required modules with minimum 50% pass rate
  • Achieve minimum 60% on final integrated assessment
  • Complete 240 hours of work-integrated learning (WIL)
  • Submit portfolio of 10+ original designs
  • Maintain good academic standing (no outstanding fees)

10.2 Certification Types

  • Full Qualification Certificate: QCTO-accredited diploma
  • Module Certificates: Individual course completion
  • Digital Badges: Skill-specific micro-credentials
  • Transcript of Records: Detailed academic performance

11. Policy Review & Updates

This Quality Policy is reviewed annually by the Academic Board in consultation with:

  • QCTO accreditation officers
  • Industry advisory board members
  • Student representative council
  • Faculty members
